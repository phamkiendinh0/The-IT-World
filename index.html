3.     Description

In this project, I will use Unity a game development software- to build the game in 2D platform.

 

In this chess game project, I would like to implement these features:

      Greeting Screen: Introduce the game to player, allow players to select game mode, learn game rules, and adjust settings.

      Gameplay mode: Introduce different difficulties, where players can select the preference level and game mode (player vs player, player vs bot).

      Timer: Time restriction on each move, and the total time a player has.

      Settings: Potentially let the player choose screen mode (window, minimized), and manipulate other elements, such as brightness, volume, etc.

      Helps: A pop-up windows that player has access to learn on using the game features.

      Game challenge: Introduce pre-arranged gameplay, challenging the player to solve the quiz with least steps and lowest timer.

      Billboard: Player ranking board in game challenge.

      Save: Allow player to save their current progress.

      Pause: Allow player to pause the game and continue when they desire.

      Exit: Allow player to close the game safely.

 

In this project, I am implementing standard features such as the settings, helps and greeting screen. Save, Pause and Exit are three mandatory functions in a game, allowing players to stop whenever they want. Player must have flexibility and control in the game, however, during a match with players, they cant pause the game until a winner is decided. These features will be implemented using Visual Studio Code, and I will use C/C++ and Python programming language to implement the logic of the game. 2.     Autonomous Vehicles

Autonomous vehicles are cars that can detect and sense the environment around it. Then, it can calculate and operate without any human intervention. A human is not required to be in the vehicle nor control the vehicle.

 

These autonomous vehicles rely on electrical components such as: switches, motion sensors, algorithms, machine learning systems, and control version to run commands. They can create and maintain a map of the cars surrounding objects with the signals respond from the sensors that are implemented across the cars gears.

 

Surrounding cars are detected with radar sensors. Video camera extracts the signals from traffic lights, observe road signs, keep in track of the other cars. It also watches out for pedestrians on the side road. All these collected signals from the sensors are then computed and calculated by the software, and executes orders to perform acceleration, braking or steering. A lot of sophisticated algorithms are used to build up such a software.


Even though it sounds fascinating, the fully automation on vehicles, or cars that offer self-driving, is not yet achieved. There are severe challenges that must be resolved in the next few years. The obstacles are:

      Weather: Heavy rains, fogs, storms, etc. are the causes that must be considered for a fully automated vehicle

      Frequency channels: Sensors and video camera communicates with the software using cables and frequency channels (Bluetooth). There must be a revolution for the frequency bandwidth, before the introduction on a huge number of autonomous vehicles.

      Accident Coverage: this is the truth that autonomous cars safety is not guarantee by anyone. If there is an accident, nobody is going to oversee the cause.

Nonetheless, the idea of autonomous vehicles is beneficial and valuable. Regardless of the negative sides, these vehicles will bring tremendous benefits to drives, such as:

      Allowing elderly and physically disabled people to be transported.

      Reduce traffic congestion: Since all vehicles are moving in a predicted pattern, they would follow the rules and respect other drivers better than human beings.

 

There are 6 levels of automation(NHTSA, n.d.) and they are:

      Level 0: The driver does all driving mechanics and actions

      Level 1: An ADAS (advanced driver assistance system) on the vehicle that sometimes assists the driver in steering or braking non-simultaneously.

      Level 2: An ADAS that can self-control both steering and braking on the car. However, it still requires the drivers attention on the driving environment, ensure distancing with other cars, watch out for pedestrians, road signs, traffic lights and traffic laws.

      Level 3: An ADS (Automated driving system) is a system that can perform all driving under certain situations. Driver must pay attention to the call of ADS when it needs human-control. Most of the time, driver still must do all the driving.

      Level 4: An ADS that can perform all the driving in certain situations. While ADS is in control, driver does not need to pay attention to the traffic

      Level 5: An ADS that can perform every action without the needs of human interaction.

Although these autonomous vehicles are far from fully completed yet, they offer such a huge impact on the future traffic system. In developing countries like Vietnam, Lao, Kampuchea, we are still operating motorcycles and other transportations along with cars. These transportations emit a huge amount of CO2 gas, forming a stiff increase in the greenhouse effect in the past few decades.


Moreover, motorcycle companies are still producing more products every year. These motorcycles are preferred because they are much more affordable comparing to cars. The peoples recognition in traffic are still low, thus, traffic is a chaos with a lot of participation from these motorcycles drivers. It will take such a long time for this situation to change.


However, with the introduction and mass production of these autonomous cars, eventually prices of the cars will drop down, and its much more affordable for people in developing countries. Cars are much more manageable than motorcycles, it will help to improve the traffic regulation and reduce traffic congestion, as well as ensuring safety for a lot of people. Automated vehicles also offer fewer human errors (drinking, phone distraction, drug abuse, etc.), therefore, it will be much safer and more accurate than a human-driver.

With the introduction of these automated vehicles, it will be much safer for everyone to participate in traffic. The price cost will potentially decline as well, offering more opportunities for families to own a car. With the number of transportations falls, greenhouse effect will be reduced tremendously, thus, saving the economy and environment simultaneously.

6.     Conclusion

From what I learnt in these resources, a game developer develops and creates source code for games. Game developers transform the game designers ideas, drawing arts, rules, gameplays, etc. into beautiful and full-functioning games.

 

Most of the time, they would be spending time to write codes those match with the design plan and idea of the Designer team. They also need to make sure that the APIs are working to enable accessibility of devices across various platforms.

 

Aside from the coding time that game developers spend on their own, they also have meetings regularly (multiple times per day) with their team and others department team (Artist, Business, Designer). They would have to discuss about the games current state, possible improvements, bug fixes, obstacles, and so much more. These meetings ensure everyone is on the same page, and the project is still on the right track. Fortunately, game developers who work in game studio dont have to negotiate with clients since they spend most of their time coding (unless they are freelancers).

Most of the game developers who work in game studio or freelancers both have flexibilities on working time. Freelancers will have more responsibilities and engagement when it comes to developing games. These programmers are called indie game developer, where they are building games either by themselves or with a small group of people. Most of the time for these developers will be involving researching, art-designing, gameplay-designing, implementation, and documentation.

On the other hand, people who work in a game studio are more specialized since they have standard regulation and policies to follow. They also take less responsibilities since the workload is distributed to all departments. Most of their time will be spent to do coding for gameplays and making sure deadlines are met. They also have meetings with other departments and group discussions regarding certain game areas for improvements, new ideas, agreement on certain aspects, etc.

There are challenges in this career field. Game studio often has the crunching phenomenon where employees are required to work overtime to ensure the deadlines are met without being compensated. This causes serious declines on the game developers general health condition and mental health. In addition, indie game developers need to have such a great creativity on their game concepts, as well as gather awareness on their product for financial income. Once a product is published, documentation is mandatory for game maintenance, updates and troubleshoot. Finally, the game project must be doable and realistic. It takes a lot of time and money investment in a game project. Thus, the game developments teams must guarantee the projects success.
